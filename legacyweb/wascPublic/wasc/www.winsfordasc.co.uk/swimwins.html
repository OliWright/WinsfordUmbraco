ASA CODES OF CONDUCT
We have, over recent weeks had a number of issues, involving swimmers and parents, which, we feel, requires the restatement of the ASA codes of conduct. These are available on the members section of our web site (column to the right), and apply to all Club sections, parents, swimmers, Teachers/coaches, and committee members.
The issues requiring a restatement of these codes include disruptive behaviour by some swimmers to our new coach, parents seeking to influence lane choice, or coaching from the balcony, and parent disagreements being aired in the changing area while children are present. We also seem to have a developing tendency to attempt to influence coaches actions by direct e-mails.
Winsford ASC has always been, and will remain a friendly club where we recognise shared ownership, and speaking up is encouraged, not discouraged.
This, however, does not give any of us licence to behave in a way which breaches the codes of conduct, or brings external parental differences into club time.
We also have a clear policy that, in employing a professional teaching and coaching team, we recognise that they take swimming decisions, on our behalf, during training and competitions. Any parent who has issues in this area should take them up with the Club Committee, not attempt to apply direct pressure.
ASA codes of conduct are, we should all be reminded, a condition of membership. Breaching them will result in action being taken which could result in exclusion from membership of our Club.

Barry Keeling, Chairman
